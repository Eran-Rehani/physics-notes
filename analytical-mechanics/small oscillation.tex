\section{Lagrangian for Small Oscillations}
The system is described by the Lagrangian:
\begin{equation}
L = T - V
\end{equation}
where \( T \) is the kinetic energy and \( V \) is the potential energy. For small oscillations around equilibrium, we define new coordinates:
\begin{equation}
q_i = \tilde{q}_i - q_{0i}, \quad \dot{q}_i = \dot{\tilde{q}}_i
\end{equation}
Expanding \( L \) to second order:
\begin{equation}
L \approx \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i,j} M_{ij} \dot{q}_i \dot{q}_j - \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i,j} K_{ij} q_i q_j
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{equation*}
M_{ij} = \frac{\partial^2 L}{\partial \dot{q}_i \partial \dot{q}_j} \Big|_{q = q_0, \dot{q} = 0}, \quad
K_{ij} = -\frac{\partial^2 L}{\partial q_i \partial q_j} \Big|_{q = q_0, \dot{q} = 0}
\end{equation*}

\section{Equations of Motion}
The equations of motion are given by:
\begin{equation}
M_{ij} \ddot{q}_j + K_{ij} q_j = 0
\end{equation}

\section{Normal Modes and Eigenfrequencies}
We assume a solution of the form:
\begin{equation}
q_i(t) = \bar{q}_i e^{i\omega t}
\end{equation}
which leads to the eigenvalue equation:
\begin{equation}
(K - \omega^2 M) \bar{q} = 0
\end{equation}
which has solutions when:
\begin{equation}
\det(K - \omega^2 M) = 0
\end{equation}
The general solution is:
\begin{equation}
q_i(t) = \sum_\ell (\bar{q}^{(\ell)})_i A_\ell \cos(\omega_\ell t + \phi_\ell)
\end{equation}

\section{Example: Two Coupled Masses on Perpendicular Tracks}
The **mass matrix**:
\begin{equation}
M = \begin{bmatrix} m & 0 \\ 0 & 2m \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}

The **stiffness matrix**:
\begin{equation}
K = \begin{bmatrix} k & -k \cos\alpha \\ -k \cos\alpha & k \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}

Solving for eigenfrequencies:
\begin{equation}
\omega^2_{\pm} = \frac{k}{4m} \left(3 \pm \sqrt{9 - 8 \sin^2 \alpha} \right)
\end{equation}

The **normal mode eigenvectors**:
\begin{equation}
\bar{q}_\pm = \begin{bmatrix} \cos\alpha \\ \frac{1}{4} (1 \mp \sqrt{9 - 8 \sin^2 \alpha}) \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}

\section{Example: CO$_2$ Molecule Vibrations}
For a CO$_2$ molecule modeled as three masses connected by springs, the **mass matrix** is:
\begin{equation}
M = \begin{bmatrix} m & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & M & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & m \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}

The **stiffness matrix**:
\begin{equation}
K = \begin{bmatrix} k & -k & 0 \\ -k & 2k & -k \\ 0 & -k & k \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}

Solving \( \det(K - \omega^2 M) = 0 \), the eigenfrequencies are:
\begin{equation}
\omega_0 = 0, \quad \omega_+ = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m} \left(1 + \frac{2m}{M} \right)}, \quad \omega_- = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
\end{equation}

\section{Example: Small Oscillations in a Power-Law Potential}
A mass \( m \) interacts with two fixed particles at \( x = \pm l \), governed by a potential:
\begin{equation}
V(r) = V_0 \left(\frac{r}{l}\right)^\alpha
\end{equation}
Expanding around equilibrium \( x = 0, y = 0 \), we find the eigenfrequencies:
\begin{equation}
\omega_1^2 = \frac{2V_0\alpha(\alpha - 1)}{ml^2}, \quad \omega_2^2 = \frac{2V_0\alpha}{ml^2}
\end{equation}

Stability conditions:
- **Stable in \( y \)-direction** for all \( \alpha > 0 \).
- **Stable in \( x \)-direction** if \( \alpha > 1 \), unstable otherwise.
